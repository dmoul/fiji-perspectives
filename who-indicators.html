5.1 Healthy life expectancy at birth (HALE)

Healthy life expectancy at birth is the “average number of years that a person can expect to live in “full health” from birth.”1.

There are some common patterns

  1. Women have a longer HALE than men in most countries.

  2. The COVID-19 pandemic reduced HALE for most countries and both sexes.

  3. “Worldwide, healthy life expectancy at birth (years) has improved by ▲ 3.57 years from 58.3 years in 2000 to 61.9 years in 2021.”2.

Unfortunately Fiji did not experience the same level of improvements.

These patterns are visible in Figure 5.1, which compares Fiji, France and USA.

Fiji is in the World Bank upper middle income group (WB_UMI: GNI per capita USD 4,516 TO 14,005 in 2023).3 In 2000, Fiji was already a lagging HALE outlier, and while the upper middle income group improved over two decades, Fiji’s HALE remained essentially unchanged, making the gap even larger (Figure 5.2).
